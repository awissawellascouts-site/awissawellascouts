Scout Promise

On my honour I promise to do my best
to do my duty to my religion and my country,
to help other people at all times
and to obey the Scout law.

"On my honour I promise to do my best"

I will try my absolute hardest in everything.

"To do my duty to my religion and my country"

I will be faithful to my faith and my nation.

"To help other people at all times"

I am always ready to help anyone in need.

"And to obey the Scout law"

I will follow the Scout rules every day.

History of the Scout Promise in Sri Lanka

Scouting in Sri Lanka began in 1912 when F.G. Stevens established the first troop in Matale. Initially, the promise followed the British version, pledging duty to the "King." However, after Sri Lanka became a Republic in 1972, the wording was changed to focus on "Religion and Country." Today, it stands as a powerful pledge that unites all Sri Lankan Scouts under one common goal of national service and spiritual growth.

How to Make the Scout Promise

When making the Scout Promise, you must stand at attention and give the Scout Sign. This is done by raising your right hand to shoulder height, holding the three middle fingers upright and the thumb over the little finger. The three fingers represent the three parts of the promise. You should speak the words clearly and solemnly, showing your personal commitment to the movement and your values.

Scout Law

(01) A Scout is Trustworthy.
(02) A Scout is Loyal.
(03) A Scout is Friendly and Considerate.
(04) A Scout is a brother to every other Scout.
(05) A Scout is Courteous.
(06) A Scout is Kind.
(07) A Scout is Obedient.
(08) A Scout is Cheerful.
(09) A Scout is Thrifty.
(10) A Scout is Clean in Thought, Word and Deed.

01. Trustworthy

A Scout's honour is to be trusted. You must be honest in everything.

02. Loyal

Be faithful to your religion, country, leaders, and friends.

03. Friendly

Be a friend to all and considerate of others' feelings.

04. Brotherly

A Scout is a brother to every other Scout in the world.

05. Courteous

Show good manners and respect to everyone, especially elders.

06. Kind

Be kind to animals and protect the natural environment.

07. Obedient

Follow the rules and obey your leaders and parents.

08. Cheerful

Stay positive and keep a smile even during difficult tasks.

09. Thrifty

Do not waste money or resources. Save for the future.

10. Clean

Be clean in your thoughts, your words, and your actions.
