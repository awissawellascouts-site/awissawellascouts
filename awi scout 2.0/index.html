what is the scouting

Scouting is a global, voluntary, non-political educational movement that provides a proven framework for character development and responsible citizenship through practical, outdoor, and adventure-based learning. It is fundamentally important because it teaches young people self-reliance, leadership, and teamwork through its unique Promise and Law and the Patrol System, ensuring they gain the essential life skills and moral compass needed to become active, capable, and ethical contributors to their local and global communities.

Credit by "world Scouting" yt channel

We believe in the vision of Lord Baden-Powell, the founder of the Scout Movement, who said, “Try and leave this world a little better than you found it.” Inspired by this guiding principle, and together with our partners across diverse spheres, we strive to deliver well-structured and value-based educational programs that nurture the physical, intellectual, emotional, social, and spiritual growth of young people. Our youth programme is designed to support a diverse range of members from the ages of 5 to 24 years, empowering them with life skills, leadership qualities, and a strong sense of service to build responsible citizens for the future.

Sigithi Scouts - First Steps

The Sigithi Scouts section is for children aged 5-7, focusing on simple activities, core values, and group play to foster curiosity and friendship.

The primary goal of Singithi Scouting in Sri Lanka is to spark an early interest in the Scout Movement by enrolling children as they begin their school journey. This program focuses on building essential competencies during the first stage of a child's development while encouraging them to love and admire nature. By engaging in simple, meaningful activities, students learn the value of helping others, allowing them to find true happiness and satisfaction through service.

Sigithi Scout Promise

“I love my Religion and Country”

Sigithi Scout Law

1. Sigithi Scout obeys elders.

2.Sigithi Scout is always happy.

To start a Singithi Scout group, first get approval from the School Principal and appoint a trained leader. Next, enroll children aged 5 to 7 (Grades 1-2) and organize them into small groups of up to 24 members. Finally, register the group with the District Scout Headquarters to receive an official number and begin fun, nature-based activities.

The Singithi Scout Uniform

The uniform is designed to be bright and comfortable for children aged 5 to 7.

  • Headwear: A Dark Blue Cap with the official Scout logo on the front.

  • Shirt: A crisp White Short-Sleeved Shirt worn by both boys and girls.

  • The Vest (Waistcoat): A unique Light Blue Vest worn over the shirt. This is the primary identifying garment for Singithi Scouts.

  • The Scarf: A Yellow Scarf tied with a woggle.

  • Placement: For boys, it goes under the shirt collar. For girls, it is typically worn over the vest.

Lower Uniform:

  • Boys: Dark Blue Shorts.

  • Girls: Dark Blue Skirt.

Footwear: Black shoes with black socks.

Children earn badges like the "Friendship Badge" through games and simple tasks.

Cub Scouts - The Adventure Begins

The Cub Scouts section is for children aged 7–11.

CFounded by Lord Baden Powell in 1916, Cub Scouting is a popular movement in Sri Lanka for children aged 7 to 11. Its mission is to educate young people through a value system based on the Scout Promise and Law, helping them become self-fulfilled individuals who play a constructive role in society. Even at the young age of seven, Cub Scouts in Sri Lanka contribute to building a better world by developing character and citizenship from early childhood.

The Cub Promise: to do their best to do their duty to God and their country, and to do a good turn every day.

Join a Pack, participate in activities as advised by the leader, and take the formal Promise after a period of training to become a full Cub Scout.

The Cub uniform includes a green cap, shirt, and shorts/skirt. The leader will guide you on the specific placement of badges.

Cub Scouts earn badges for various skills (e.g., swimming, reading, gardening). These are typically worn on the arm or shirt pocket.

Junior Scouts operate in small teams (Patrols), training them to become self-sufficient and responsible for their activities.

Emphasizes the importance of living by the Scout Law and Promise, and the principles of service to the community.

After initial training, Scouts take the formal Oath and Promise to be invested as a Scout.

The uniform consists of a Khaki shirt and blue or Khaki shorts/trousers, which may vary by country.

Scouts earn Proficiency Badges and Rank Badges. These are worn on the arm and the left shirt pocket.

Senior Scouts (age 14.5–18) focus on advanced leadership roles, adventurous journeys, and high-level training to prepare for adult life.

Senior Scouts act as youth leaders, planning and executing their own programs with guidance from adult leaders.

The Promise is renewed, focusing on applying the principles to societal challenges and leadership responsibilities.

Achieve advanced training levels and participate in national and international activities based on modern scouting methods.

The standard Scout uniform, often with special shoulder badges to denote senior ranks.

Rover Scouts - The Pathfinders

Rover Scouts is the young adult section (age 17.5–26). Service to the community, personal development, and social responsibility are central to their activities.

About
Law & Promise
How to Start
Uniform

Rover Scouts form self-governing groups (Crews) and focus on service projects, living by the motto "Service."

The Law and Promise are used as guidelines for tackling modern-day challenges and making ethical decisions in adult life.

After progressing from the Senior Scout section or joining as a new member, the candidate undergoes training as a "Rover Squire."

The uniform includes the standard Scout attire along with special Rover Scarf and specific shoulder flashes.

They earn the Rover Badge and other badges related to community service and adventure projects.

Scout Leaders

Dedicated volunteers who guide, mentor, and support the entire Scouting movement across all age sections (Age over 18).

Scout Leaders are committed adult volunteers who make a positive impact on the lives of young people.

Leaders are expected to exemplify the Scout Law and Promise, serving as role models for all scouts.

Register as an adult member and complete the necessary leadership training, typically culminating in the Wood Badge.

The leader uniform varies by section and national rules, with the Wood Badge being the most significant honour.

Badges and awards are given for various levels of leadership, service, and advanced training courses.

The Scout Programme, which includes Cub Sigithi, Cub Scouts, Junior Scouts, Senior Scouts, and Rover Scouts, is designed to develop young people into disciplined, confident, and responsible individuals through age-appropriate activities, teamwork, outdoor experiences, leadership training, and community service, guided by the Scout Promise and Law.
